About Me

I'm a fourth-year Echols Scholar (undergraduate) at the University of Virginia, actively involved in research. Currently, I am a member of UVA-MLSys where I work with Professor Judy Fox for the "AI For Science" project, particularly focusing on Interpretable Time Series and Sensitivity Analysis Method. Additionally, I am an undergraduate researcher at the Information and Language Processing (ILP) Lab at UVA led by Professor Yangfeng Ji, in which I am working towards a publication centered on the Consistency of Large Language Models' political leaning.

My research in centered on the Reliability and Interpretability of modern Deep Learning Models dealing with Sequential Data, such as human language and time-series data. Additionally, I am very proud to have been a Teaching Assistant in Introduction to Data Science with Python, under phD candidate Sydney Campbell.

As I approach graduation, I am planning to pursue a graduate's program in Computer Science or in related field such as Electrical Engineering. I am proficient in Python, Java, C, and assembly (x86) language. I speak English, Chinese, and French.
